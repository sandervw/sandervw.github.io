General Edication Reflection

While many of the general education electives I took had no direct application to the field of software engineering, I feel as though they still had an affect on the overall direction I took in the field, and on how I approach problems in software engineering. The most significant way in which many of these courses affected my career in software engineering, was that they made me thankful for choosing SE of them. I only say this half-jokingly, as many of these classes, though fun and informative, were structured strangely or did not hold my attention as well as courses in my major. In this way, many of these courses affirmed my choice in software engineering as a major, something that I believe was needed during my freshman and sophomore years.

I would say that of the courses listed above, my favorites were the history classes. I enjoyed learning about other cultures and locations in particular, and believe these have had a direct affect on my plans after college. I plan on trying to obtain a job outside of Iowa, or even outside of the US after college, in no small part because these electives instilled in interest in other cultures.

I would say that a common weakness of these courses is that they do not have much application when it comes to transferring problem solving abilities learned to an engineering environment. This, i believe, is because many of these are not problem solving classes. They introduce new cultures, ideas, values, languages, etc... but they rarely involved solving some sort of problem in a way that I was not already familiar with. Many of them focused more on essays and reading assignments, things I was already quite familiar with. I do not think that this devalues these courses. On the contrary, I believe that it was often good to have some of these courses as a sort of reprieve from the problem solving focus of many software engineering courses. I apologize if this answer seems somewhat opposing to what was asked for in the outline, but it is how I feel. I do believe that these courses were an overall benefit to my education.

Prior Work

- CPRE 185: 2D Game Project

For this project, I worked on creating a 2D Game that served as our major project throughout the second half of the semester. This was a two person project, and was thus not expected to be very large or function-filled, but it was required that we get as much of it working as possible. My partner in the project dropped out of the class not long after it started, so I ended up doing the project by myself. The project was built entirely using C++ and a graphics library designed by the instructor for the class. It was not a large library, and mostly consisted of functions for drawing simple shapes, and some functions to load .jpg image files. The class was my first programming programming experience ever, and it might be one of my favorite programming experience. The instructor was very passionate about his job and starting off my programming experience by building a game was tons of fun. The demo and code involved are long lost, but I still Appreciate the knowledge and skills I gained in that class.

Cumulative Reflection

Over the course of my education here at Iowa State University, I have become acquainted with and worked on technologies of various complexities and usefulnesses. I have enjoyed working with and learning about most of these technologies, and believe that the knowledge I have gained will help me in my career as a programmer and Software Engineer. The complex technologies and classes that I gained experience with will likely prove invaluable when/if I encounter them in the future.

My work in groups and teams of various sizes has already aided me when working in a job environment, and made it much easier to communicate with people both inside and out of my field when working in groups both large and small. While working in these groups, I was often introduced to methods and programs that were not presented in class, but that proved to be useful both in the projects and outside of them. Overall, my work in groups has been one of my favorite parts of my education here at Iowa State, both because of the people I worked with, and because of the new and innovative technologies I was introduced to.

Overall, I was very pleased with my experience here at Iowa State in the Software Engineering Program. If I were to go back through it again, I might have attempted to pay more attention in the first few years of my education, but I do not think a lack of attention in my first few classes ended up being hugely detrimental to my education as a whole. I also did not participate in many student organizations, career fairs, or research assitanceships, which might have been interesting or helpful outside of the classroom. Quite often, however, I do apply some of what I have learned at iowa state to my current job, whether it means developing code/documents, or working with other professesional in my company. In the end, I believe I have been a successful student.
