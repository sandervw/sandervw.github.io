General Education Reflection

While many of the general education electives I took had no direct application to the field of software engineering, I feel as though they still had an affect on the overall direction I took in the field, and on how I approach problems in software engineering. The most significant way in which many of these courses affected my career in software engineering, was that they made me thankful for choosing SE of them. I only say this half-jokingly, as many of these classes, though fun and informative, were structured strangely or did not hold my attention as well as courses in my major. In this way, many of these courses affirmed my choice in software engineering as a major, something that I believe was needed during my freshman and sophomore years. I also believe that it was often good to have some of these courses as a sort of reprieve from the problem solving focus of many software engineering courses. I do believe that these courses were, in the end, an overall benefit to my education.

Often while working on problems in Engineering, I will use things that I learned during my general elective courses. Software Engineering courses have a focus on problem solving most of the time, but general elective courses (the ones I took anyway) tend to help more study techniques, paper-composition, and research oriented learning. As an example: During one of my group project classes, S E 329, we were required to research a method of using facial recognition software, design a program that recognized faces, and write up a report about it. The designing of the program was something I was familiar from software engineering, and I probably could have accomplished the other two parts with only minor trouble, but the techniques I had learned for report composition and research in my general education classes made it easier to finish those parts of the project in a shorter amount of time. This is just one example of how my general elective courses have helped me.

After college, I plan on working for a few years to pay off any remaing student loan debts. I have a bit of job security currently, as I believe that the company that I am working for will offer me full time employment at graduation. I still plan on attending the upcoming career fair and perhaps going to a few interviews in the event that there is an open job that really interests me. Within the next 5 to 10 years, I would like to have any remaining student loan debts paid off, and I would like to be moved out of the state of Iowa. I do not dislike Iowa, but I would like to try living in a larger City in a different state at soem point, if only for the experience it offers.

Raspberry Pi Smart Home Senior Design Project

Our project for senior design is to enhance an existing smart home system for our client, Ryan Arndorfer. He has a basic setup with Raspberry Pi Modules, but wants us to design a chip/power supply layout to reduce space, and a web app to control lights and other electrical devices on the system. My role in the project is lead Software Engineer. I have been working on designing the web app that will connect to a web server on the system. I have learned a few new things about security, and a lot of new info on Raspberry Pi Modules, something that I did not have much experience with before. All of our documentation is on the Senior Design Website, which I have linked below.

Senior Design Website (Password = "2017yesterDayCenter15%&")

Though I do not think that home automation has a great many application at solving global problems, I do believe that the system our client wants to implement is much simpler than other home automation solutions that currently exist, based on the research we have done. His system is cheaper, but it does not give up functionality present in other systems. It does this by having most of the modules wired together with an ethernet cord, as opposed to connection via BlueTooth or wifi. This saves space and money when implementing the home automation system.

Cumulative Reflection

Over the course of my education here at Iowa State University, I have become acquainted with and worked on technologies of various complexities and usefulnesses. I have enjoyed working with and learning about most of these technologies, and believe that the knowledge I have gained will help me in my career as a programmer and Software Engineer. The complex technologies and classes that I gained experience with will likely prove invaluable when/if I encounter them in the future. In additions, the subject of ethics has been an aspect of many classes I have taken in the Software Engineering classes I have taken. Though actual ethical guidelines (such as those defined by ABET or IEEE) have been explained in these classes, I do not feel that following ethics has been an issue I have had, before or after my time here at ISU.

My work in groups and teams of various sizes has already aided me when working in a job environment, and made it much easier to communicate with people both inside and out of my field when working in groups both large and small. While working in these groups, I was often introduced to methods and programs that were not presented in class, but that proved to be useful both in the projects and outside of them. Overall, my work in groups has been one of my favorite parts of my education here at Iowa State, both because of the people I worked with, and because of the new and innovative technologies I was introduced to.

Overall, I was very pleased with my experience here at Iowa State in the Software Engineering Program. If I were to go back through it again, I might have attempted to pay more attention in the first few years of my education, but I do not think a lack of attention in my first few classes ended up being hugely detrimental to my education as a whole. I also did not participate in many student organizations, career fairs, or research assitanceships, which might have been interesting or helpful outside of the classroom. Quite often, however, I do apply some of what I have learned at iowa state to my current job, whether it means developing code/documents, or working with other professesional in my company. In the end, I believe I have been a successful student.
